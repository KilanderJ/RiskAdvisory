Questions related to LCR

LCR assets can be divided into three main categories to which different hair-cuts are applied
  • Level 1A - Haircut 0%: Coins and physical money, exposures to central banks within EEA or a national government, exposures to municipalitites or multilateral banks, and exposures which have explicit guarantees from any of these.
  • Level 1B - haircut 7%: Covered bonds which have a minimum issued amount of 500 MEUR and AAA-rating.
  • Level 2A - haircut 15%: Covered bonds which have a minimum issued amount of 250 MEUR and AAA-rating.
  • Level 2B - haircut 50%: Bonds issued by Corporates with a minimum issued amount of 250 MEUR and minimum official rating of BBB-.
Of the total amount used for liquidity coverage a minimum of 30% must be made up of Level 1A assets, 1B must not acount for more than 70% and Level 2A + 2B must not account for more than 40%.

Certificated can not be repoed in the Riksbank but should be able to qualify anyway as they can be sold bilaterally and coverted to cash.

There are a number of formal requirements and they are listed in Bilaga H4 on the Riksbank webpage: Bilaga H4. However the most important are:
  • Lowest official rating of AA- from either S&P, Moody or Fitch
  • Listed on a regulated market in an approved jurisdiction
  • Outstanding issued amount of at least 100 MSEK

Questions related to Basel III Risk Weights

The general rules for setting risk weights according to the IRB Foundational approach (Schablonmetoden) are as follows:
  • Government with ratings AAA 0%
  • Government with ratings AA 20%
  • Exposures to municipalities shall be treated as exposure to goverment if on this list. In practice all Swedish municipalities.
  • Exposures to multilateral banks 0%
  • Exposures to financial institutions which are deemed NOT to have an official rating:
    • National government AAA => 20% risk weight for financial institution (relevant for Sweden)
    • National government AA => 50% risk weight for financial institution
    In practice the first rule of 20% is applied by most Savings Banks.
  • Exposures to financial institutions which are deemed to have a offical rating:
    • AAA: 20%
    • AA: 50%
    • A: 50%
    • BBB: 100%
  • Exposure to corporates without official rating is set to 100% (or the risk weight for the national government which ever is the highest). For Swedish companies this implies a risk weight of 100%.
  • Exposures to corporates with offical ratings:
    • AAA: 20%
    • AA: 50%
    • A: 100%
    • BBB: 100%
    • BB: 100%
    • B and below: 150%
  • Exposures to covered bonds with official rating:
    • AAA: 10%
    • AA: 20%
    • A: 20%
    • BBB: 50%

The impact on risk weights from buying hybrid bonds issued by a non-financial corporate, e.g. Telia, Vattenfall etc., is no different from buying any other bond. Buying subordinated bonds isued by a financial institution, e.g. a bank or an isurance company, has a detrimental impact on regulatory bank capital as the amount invested has to be deducted from the capital base.
